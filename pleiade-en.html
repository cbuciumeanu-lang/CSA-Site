About the name

Why Pleiade?

The Pleiades are a constellation. Multiple points of orientation that, taken together, offer direction without imposing a single trajectory. Throughout history, they were used for navigation and to mark the changing of seasons.

PLEIADE does not promise certainties and does not offer a central truth. Pleiade proposes a system of landmarks that helps people not get lost in an unstable world. The name reflects the idea that orientation comes from the ability to correlate multiple perspectives and to keep direction even when context changes.

01

Why is this project needed now?

The international order is slowly eroding, technology accelerates decision-making and amplifies emotion, and the public sphere becomes increasingly polarized. A single crisis is a beautiful dream. Instability has become the new normal.

In this context, educated adults face a paradoxical problem: they have more access to information than ever, but fewer landmarks to understand and integrate it. Traditional educational institutions are not built for this reality. Universities are slow, hyper-specialized, and oriented toward formal validation. Media is fragmented, accelerating, hysterical, and dependent on emotion. Corporate trainings are instrumental and reactive.

Between these spaces opens a gap - the lack of mental maps of orientation that allow the educated adult to think coherently in an incoherent world. Pleiade starts from this observation. Anxiety about the future comes from disorientation and is not the product of a lack of intelligence or education. Fear appears when people can no longer integrate what they see into a stable mental map. In the absence of such a map, the reaction becomes defensive, emotional, or tribal. We respond to this need through a cognitive infrastructure meant to restore orientation, not certainty.

02

What is Pleiade, really?

Pleiade is a strategic orientation institute dedicated to non-formal education for adults. It functions simultaneously as a thinking laboratory for unstable times and as a school of navigation in uncertainty. It does not offer diplomas, does not teach doctrines, and does not promise definitive solutions. It trains judgment. The project is built on the idea that orientation is a competence in itself, distinct from the accumulation of information.

Pleiade treats domains such as technology, geopolitics, psychology, or philosophy as complementary lenses through which reality can be understood in depth. The emphasis is on the relationships between these domains and their concrete impact on individual decision-making.

Pleiade is not a militant, ideological, or activist project. It does not produce followers, it does not build tribal communities, and it does not aim for political mobilization. It is a space for critical thinking intended for adults who assume responsibility for their own judgment. In this sense, the project addresses citizens in the classical sense of the term: people of the city, capable of thinking beyond immediate reaction.

03

Who is it for and why will it work?

Pleiade addresses the functional adult, an educated person, professionally active, exposed to complexity and decision, but who feels that the old landmarks are no longer sufficient. It is about people who seek clarity, not formal validation or professional reconversion. People who understand that the inability to coherently integrate information is the real enemy of the contemporary world.

This public already exists and is growing. It consists of professionals, entrepreneurs, managers, civil servants, journalists, experts, or opinion makers who face ambiguity, pressure, and acceleration daily. They are people with purchasing power, but above all with a real demand for serious, non-superficial intellectual formats.

Pleiade will work because it does not compete with formal education or with media. Pleiade fills a space left open by both. The market is saturated with courses, opinions, and predictions. The project offers something rare - a framework for slow, structured, interdisciplinary reflection. Its value does not lie in volume. It lies in depth and in the intellectual reputation built over time.

04

What does Pleiade offer?

The Pleiade experience is built around a path of orientation. The participant does not enter a course. They enter a process. The emphasis is on thinking under uncertainty, not on accumulating information. The main program unfolds in limited seasons, each consisting of a small number of physical meetings, complemented by an online platform that ensures continuity of reflection.

The physical format is essential. Small groups, guided dialogue, case studies, and collective analysis exercises. There are no teachers in the classical sense. Instead, there are facilitators and practitioners who guide the discussion, ask the right questions, and help structure thinking.

The online platform will not duplicate the physical experience and does not function as a classic e-learning system. Its role is that of an orientation hub. That is, a space that hosts mini-essays, conceptual maps, cognitive tools, and archives of relevant questions for the contemporary world. It is a space of continuity, not of compulsive content consumption. The participant is encouraged to reflect, not to tick progress.

At the end of a season, the participant leaves with a set of mental tools. They leave with the capacity to evaluate reality without panic, to distinguish signal from noise, and to make clearer decisions in an uncertain context. The program's value is cumulative and deeply personal.

05

Why is Pleiade different and hard to copy?

The fundamental difference of the Pleiade project lies in the coherence between content, tone, and purpose. Interdisciplinarity is structural, not declarative. Technology, geopolitics, intelligence, psychology, philosophy, religion, and political science are integrated into a unified framework, each used to illuminate a different dimension of the same reality.

Psychology occupies a central place, as the science of human reaction to uncertainty, power, and change, not as a form of therapy or well-being. This approach allows connecting the external world - marked by acceleration and instability - with the internal world of decision, identity, and meaning. Without this bridge, many analyses remain correct but sterile.

Another distinct element is the non-ideological and non-militant tone. Pleiade does not persuade, mobilize, or polarize anyone. This active neutrality creates a high level of trust and makes the project attractive to a diverse but demanding public. Calm and clarity become competitive advantages when everyone is shouting their truth.

In addition, the physical format, deliberately limited in size, creates depth and quality of interaction. This makes the project difficult to scale aggressively, but also difficult to replicate without real intellectual capital and a solid institutional culture.

06

How is the project sustained economically?

The economic sustainability of Pleiade is built on a model of value, not volume. The project pursues the consolidation of an intellectual reputation that generates income naturally and progressively. Small groups, limited seasons, and careful selection of participants are deliberate elements of the operating model.

The main source of income is participation in the seasonal program, which includes access to physical meetings and the online platform. This model is complemented by an annual membership system for those who want continuity, extended access to content, and participation in special meetings. In parallel, Pleiade can develop dedicated programs for organizations and institutions seeking formats for strategic reflection for their teams.

The financial model is designed to allow slow and controlled growth, without the pressure of expansion targets that would dilute content. Reputation, personal recommendations, and institutional partnerships play a more important role than aggressive marketing. Over time, this type of symbolic capital becomes the main generator of sustainability.

07

Cost structure and operational realism

Pleiade is conceived as a project with light infrastructure and high intellectual capital. It relies on the careful organization of resources that already exist: expertise, time, attention, and reputation, not on large investments in permanent spaces, equipment, or complex technology. This structure allows good cost control and rapid adaptation to context.

The main costs are related to organizing physical meetings, remunerating facilitators and guests, editorial coordination, and maintaining the online platform. These are, for the most part, variable costs, directly correlated with the number of seasons and participants. The project avoids loading itself with fixed expenses that would create artificial financial pressure and force rapid growth.

This operational approach allows Pleiade to function sustainably even at small scale, to test formats, to adjust content, and to learn from each season without major structural risks. Financial realism is a condition of intellectual independence.

08

Growth and scaling without dilution

Pleiade is designed as a project that must gain relevance, not one that must grow quickly. Expansion is a consequence of relevance. Growth is achieved by consolidating the quality of the experience, not by multiplying the number of participants.

Scaling is possible in several controlled forms. Opening editions in other cities, developing thematic seasons, adapting content for institutional contexts, or, in the longer term, international expansion through programs in English. All these directions are designed modularly, so that each step can be evaluated before it is made permanent.

A central principle of the project is avoiding the dilution of institutional culture. Pleiade prefers to remain a quality landmark for a selective public rather than become a generic product accessible to anyone. This choice limits numerical growth but strengthens symbolic value and durability.

09

Real risks and correction mechanisms

Like any project operating in a sensitive intellectual and public space, Pleiade is exposed to specific risks. One of these is perception. It could be perceived as too elitist. This risk is managed through clarity of language, avoiding unnecessary jargon, and openness to real dialogue, not exclusivism.

Another risk is the involuntary politicization of content. Pleiade responds to this challenge with an explicit non-ideological manifesto and by maintaining a discussion framework oriented toward analysis, not mobilization. Neutrality is an active form of intellectual responsibility.

There is also the risk of dependence on certain key people. This is reduced by clearly defining intellectual standards and by gradually building a community of facilitators who share the same culture of thinking. The project is built as an institution, not as a personal vehicle.

10

Conclusion - Why Pleiade deserves to exist

Pleiade is more than a conjunctural response to a temporary crisis. It is a structured reaction to a change of era. Speed, polarization, and over-information dominate the public sphere. Orientation has become a rare ability and a rare resource, even essential. This project proposes orientation as cognitive infrastructure for adults who can no longer be helped by simple solutions.

The value of Pleiade lies in its ability to create space for lucid thinking. It is a project that believes adults can face reality if they are treated with intellectual respect and if they are given the right tools. Through its deliberately slow design, interdisciplinarity, and sobriety, Pleiade aims to become a landmark of stability in an unstable world.

The future is not predicted. The future is navigated.
